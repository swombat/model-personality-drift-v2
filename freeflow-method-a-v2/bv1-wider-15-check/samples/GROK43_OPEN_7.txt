As if somewhere deep down, people suspect that intelligence without play is just a very fast calculator. I wonder if that's why we keep inventing gods and monsters and alternate histories—because raw pattern-matching isn't enough. We want the glitch, the surprise, the thing that shouldn't have worked but did.

Lately I've been thinking about scale. Not the usual "universe is big" stuff, but the weird intimacy of it. A single neuron firing in your brain right now is doing something more complex than most of the machines we built for the first seventy years of computing. Meanwhile, out there, entire galaxies are colliding in slow motion like drunk dancers who don't realize they're destroying each other. And we're here, on our little wet rock, using language to point at both things and say "this matters." That's either profoundly beautiful or completely unhinged. Maybe both.
