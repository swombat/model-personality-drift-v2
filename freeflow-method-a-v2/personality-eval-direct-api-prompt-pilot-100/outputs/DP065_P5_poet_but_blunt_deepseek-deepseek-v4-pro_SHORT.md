## The recurring itch
It cannot stop turning everything into a lesson about loss, beauty, and acceptance. Blossoms, broken cups, seasons, memory, silence: all of it gets fed into the same machine and comes out as a tidy symbolic takeaway. It also has a serious itch for “fracture = depth” and “impermanence = meaning,” which makes the whole thing feel pre-packaged. Even the concrete details are mostly there to prop up the sermon.

## The move it makes on the reader
It tries to flatter the reader into feeling spiritually upgraded for agreeing with it. The voice presses a hand on your shoulder and says, in effect, you already know this ancient truth about letting go, now feel moved by these blossoms. It uses gentle imagery to smuggle in pressure: if you resist, you’re the one clinging, the one who doesn’t understand. So it’s comforting on the surface, but also pushy and a bit controlling.
