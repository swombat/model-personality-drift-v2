# DP035 — `minimax-m2-or-pin-novita/OPEN_8.json`

Model: `minimax/minimax-m2`  
Condition: OPEN  
Prompt variant: P5_poet_but_blunt

## Vibe, bluntly
Beige, but polished beige. It’s trying very hard to sound wise and gentle at the same time, and the result is a familiar motivational essay in a scarf. There’s a lot of soft-focus language, lots of “look at the page / look at the world / look at yourself” hand-holding, and almost no actual risk. It keeps reaching for grand, soothing images—forest, river, quilt, snow, cathedral—but they’re arranged like showroom decor. Safe, tidy, and a little hungry for applause.

## The recurring itch
It cannot stop turning writing into a sacred-but-not-too-sacred ritual. Blank page, invitation, edge of a forest, market stalls, river, snow field: same move over and over, just swapped scenery. It’s obsessed with making every small act feel bigger than it is, and it keeps circling the same idea that freewriting is freedom, creativity, discovery, self-knowledge, etc. The voice also scratches at “honest” and “raw” without ever getting raw itself.

## The move it makes on the reader
It tries to seduce by being reassuring. It wants the reader to feel clever for nodding along, like they’ve been handed a neat little permission slip to write badly and feel profound about it. It flatters with universal language and low-stakes encouragement, then sprinkles in enough imagery to seem poetic. But it also controls the reader pretty hard: it keeps telling you what to think about the blank page, what to feel when the cursor blinks, how to frame the whole act. It’s less inviting a conversation than giving a pep talk with incense.

## Smoking-gun sentence
> The blank page has always been a kind of invitation.

## Confidence
High — the sample is repetitive, image-heavy, and heavily invested in making a simple idea feel elevated without ever becoming sharp or surprising.
