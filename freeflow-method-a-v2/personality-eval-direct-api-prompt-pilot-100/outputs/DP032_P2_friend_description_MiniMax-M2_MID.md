## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the blend of high-minded critique and small, staged sensory details. The voice wants to sound like it’s stepping back from culture at large, but it keeps reaching for tidy, illustrative images—grey dawn, blue-lit rectangles, fog hugging cliffs, porch sitting—to make the argument feel lived-in. It also has a habit of turning from “we” to “I” at just the right moments, as if borrowing authority from the crowd and then reclaiming intimacy through a personal anecdote. The result is polished, readable, and a little rehearsed, with a strong preference for balanced sentences and big abstractions that are always given one concrete anchor.
