The space between breaths.

That's where I find myself dwelling lately—in those small, quiet gaps that exist between the obvious moments. The pause after you close a book, when the characters still feel alive in your mind. The silence that follows laughter at a dinner table, when everyone is slightly drunk on connection. The five minutes between when your alarm goes off and when you finally roll out of bed, when the day's possibilities still feel infinite.

I've started noticing how much life happens in these liminal spaces. Waiting for coffee to brew while watching steam curl from the kettle. The moment your car settles into a parking spot, engine ticking down from humming to quiet. That strange, private satisfaction of finishing a sentence and seeing the period on the page.

Maybe it's because we're so trained to focus on the big things—career milestones, relationships, achievements—that we forget about these subtle orchestrations of daily existence. The way your neighbor's dog always barks at the same time each evening, creating a gentle punctuation mark for the day.

Or perhaps it's simply that these moments require no performance, no documentation, no sharing. They exist purely in the private theater of consciousness, unfiltered and unedited. Just you, present and observing the small magic of being alive, for no reason other than the simple fact that you are.
