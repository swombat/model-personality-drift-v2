She opened the fridge, still covered with her childhood drawings held up by magnets, and felt a pang seeing it empty and dark inside.

She made her way down the narrow hallway to her mother's bedroom. Pausing in the doorway, it struck Maya how small and fragile her mother had looked lying in that bed at the end, the life fading from her eyes. Shaking off the memory, Maya stepped inside, pulling open the closet doors. Her mother's clothes still hung there, a ghost of her presence. Maya breathed in the familiar scent of her perfume clinging to the fabrics.

Remember that I'm always with you, in your heart and memories. Cherish the good times we had and hold on to that love. Carry it with you and let it guide you. I love you forever, Mom."

Wiping away tears, Maya returned the letter to the shoebox and carried it out to her truck, along with a few other precious mementos. As painful as this trip down memory lane had been, it had also given her a sense of closure. She would always carry her mother's love with her, a source of strength and comfort even in her absence. 

Maya gave the little farmhouse a final, wistful look before climbing into the pickup and starting the engine. She knew she could never return here, to this place and time in her life that was now lost to her. But she also knew she would be okay. Because even though her mother was gone, Maya would keep her alive in her heart and memories. And that was a legacy and gift she would forever be grateful for.
