## What they would probably talk about again
They’d keep returning to sacrifice, voice, silence, and the price of speaking. Not as abstract themes, but as bodily things: breath leaving you, light leaving a wrist, words as coins you spend or hoard. They like systems where emotion has visible mechanics—counters, clocks, limits, glowing numbers—because then every feeling can be dramatized as a choice. I’d expect them to revisit lonely prodigies, forbidden connection, people who live too carefully versus people who burn too fast, and the idea that being heard is worth damage. They also seem fond of first contact scenes where one person teaches another how to see the world again.

## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the relentless conversion of feeling into visible depletion. Everything in this piece has a meter on it: speech, life, courage, even intimacy. That gives the writing a very particular pressure-cooker rhythm, where every line is doing two jobs at once—moving the scene and spending something. The voice loves clean oppositions, but it keeps dressing them in lush language: quiet/noisy, full/empty, ghost/body, hoard/spend, winter/fire. It also has a habit of making one sentence stand up like a stage monologue—short, quotable, almost ceremonial—right in the middle of all the ornate setup. The result is earnest, highly legible, and a little addicted to the big emotional turn.
