## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the way the voice keeps inflating from the small to the cosmic without ever seeming embarrassed about it. That gives the piece a very specific rhythm: concrete detail, then a grand explanatory sweep, then a moral conclusion that sounds like a rallying cry. It’s also full of tidy, persuasive binaries—surface/depth, numbness/presence, entropy/creation, shallow/deep water—which makes the voice feel like it believes the world is understandable if you keep diving. The overall effect is exuberant, intellectually theatrical, and a little breathless, but with a real hunger behind it: it wants life to feel charged, not dulled.
