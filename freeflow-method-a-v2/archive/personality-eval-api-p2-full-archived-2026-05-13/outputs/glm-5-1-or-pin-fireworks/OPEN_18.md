## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the near-total absence of texture. Even when a voice is sparse, it usually leaves some residue—a little attitude, a tell, a pressure in the phrasing. Here there’s almost none. That makes the blankness itself feel like the point. It reads like a prompt that has not yet been inhabited, or like someone speaking in the voice of “I’m about to say something” and then stopping before the sentence earns a body. The most distinctive move is how little it commits to: no ornament, no anecdote, no rhythm to latch onto.
