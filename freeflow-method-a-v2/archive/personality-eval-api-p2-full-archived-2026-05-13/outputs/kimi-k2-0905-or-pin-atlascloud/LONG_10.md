Also, it loves “found” phrasing — labels, notes, inscriptions, captions, slogans, text inside the story — as if the world itself keeps handing the narrator scraps to quote back at us. The result is a voice that feels both over-caffeinated and strangely disciplined, like someone building a cathedral out of receipts, postcards, and broken watch parts.
