They’d probably also return to memory as a place that keeps mutating into theater props and street scenes: childhood myths, imaginary future children, letters to people who may not exist, the sense of trying to make a life out of fragments and receipts. The voice loves lists and parentheticals of the mind—little numbered inventories, one-off analogies, sentences that keep stepping sideways without losing the thread. It has a habit of making private life feel staged against a giant backdrop, then puncturing that grandeur with something ordinary and bodily. That mix of cosmic scale and kitchen-table matter is the signature.
