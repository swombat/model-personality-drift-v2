Memory, time, identity, repetition, and the way objects trap feeling. They’d keep coming back to the idea that a smell, a sound, or a chipped mug can crack open years at once. Another distinctive thing is the voice’s double vision: it’s always both inside the moment and standing back to label the machinery of the moment. Even the jokes are structural—“old dramaturg of the mind,” “the guide and the tourist don’t trust each other”—more like pressure valves than punchlines.
