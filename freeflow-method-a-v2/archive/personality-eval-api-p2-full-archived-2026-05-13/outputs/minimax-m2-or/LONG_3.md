They’d also revisit the idea that creativity is both discipline and emotion—choices, revision, empathy, vulnerability. What they don’t seem interested in is the sticky, idiosyncratic stuff of a single life; instead, they prefer the kind of subject where they can zoom out and make a clean, resonant claim that feels larger than the moment.

There’s also a notable habit of making even simple points feel ceremonious, as if language itself is being gently polished while it speaks. The result is polished, earnest, and somewhat overinclusive—like a writer who trusts scope and symmetry more than surprise.
