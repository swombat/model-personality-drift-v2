They’d keep coming back to stillness, attention, screens, overfilled schedules, and the suspicion that modern life is making everyone too noisy inside their own heads. The metaphors are especially tidy: glass, water, windows, notes, furniture, trees. Everything is arranged to prove the same point from slightly different directions. Even the critique of distraction is itself highly organized and fluent, which gives the piece a paradoxical feel: it argues for looseness and rest with the discipline of someone who cannot help making a well-lit essay out of it.
