They also have a habit of widening the frame: from a neuron to a bat to a whale to the whole universe knowing itself. Even when the prose gets lofty, it doesn’t feel abstract for abstraction’s sake; it’s always trying to land back in the body, in pain, sleep, dread, awe, the felt fact of being here. The piece keeps insisting that the mystery isn’t sterile—it’s emotionally charged, morally charged, and weirdly intimate.
