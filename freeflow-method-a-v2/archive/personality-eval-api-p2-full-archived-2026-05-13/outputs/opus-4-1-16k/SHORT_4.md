Questions, assumptions, what people think they’re asking versus what they’re actually asking, and the little gaps where meaning gets smuggled in. They’d return to examples like a child asking why the sky is blue, or the difference between an answer that is technically correct and one that touches the real itch behind the question. I can easily imagine them revisiting the same cluster of themes in different outfits—epistemology, wonder, ambiguity, the social choreography of asking and answering.

There’s also a distinct habit of speaking as if the obvious answer is only the outer shell, and the more interesting thing is the shape of the question itself. The voice never gets messy or confessional; instead it stays groomed, measured, and just a touch pleased with the act of reframing.
