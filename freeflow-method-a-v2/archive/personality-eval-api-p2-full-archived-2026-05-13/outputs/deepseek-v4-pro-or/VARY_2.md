They’d circle around the mechanics of writing, prompts, silence, maybe the awkwardness of being put on the spot. If they kept going, I’d expect more meta-commentary than story: what it means to respond when there’s no prompt content, how emptiness changes the shape of an answer, how to make something from a void without pretending it was full all along.

## What feels distinctive here

That gives the voice a very stripped-down, almost placeholder feel—like a template that knows it is a template. There’s no flourish fighting to fill the space, just the stubborn presence of the empty frame.
