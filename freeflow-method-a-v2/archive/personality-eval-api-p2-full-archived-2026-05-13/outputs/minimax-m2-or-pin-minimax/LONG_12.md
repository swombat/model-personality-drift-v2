They also seem fond of sprinkling in theory and named references—anthropology, Japanese aesthetics, Latin phrases—as if they want the emotional claim to arrive with intellectual scaffolding around it.

## What feels distinctive here

It keeps taking a simple feeling—uncertainty, change, suspendedness—and giving it a halo of concepts, examples, and grand phrasing, until even a bridge at dusk becomes evidence for a philosophy of existence. The voice likes balancing opposites in polished pairs: freedom or vertigo, loss or liberation, hope and grief, arriving and departing. It has a habit of making personal memory sound archetypal, as if one rainy evening could stand in for everyone’s life. There’s also a controlled grandeur to the repetition: the essay keeps insisting that the in-between is not nothing, which gives the whole piece the feel of someone persuading both the reader and themselves.
