## What feels distinctive here

It doesn’t just describe rain; it turns rain into threads, lanterns, percussion, a mirror, a metaphor for writing, a metaphor for memory, and a metaphor for life all at once. The sentences have that gently swelling, polished cadence where everything gets translated into something lyrical and emotionally tidy. There’s also a particular habit of making the speaker sound solitary but not starkly so—alone in a tea shop, but never empty, because the room is crowded with reflection, recollection, and significance. It’s the voice of someone who believes scenes are there to be interpreted, not just witnessed.
