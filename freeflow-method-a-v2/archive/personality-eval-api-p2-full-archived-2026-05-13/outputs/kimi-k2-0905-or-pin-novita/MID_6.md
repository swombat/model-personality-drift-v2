## What feels distinctive here

The voice loves a neat turn—“memory loops forward,” “déjà vu is only good lighting,” “grief is only love with nowhere to go”—and then backs it up with something tactile and oddly specific so it doesn’t float away. It also has this habit of treating thought as something physical: blinking, tasting, humming, cracking, carrying. Even the emotions arrive through objects and surfaces rather than direct announcement. The result is a voice that feels both lush and tightly controlled, as if it’s building a little private cosmology out of crumbs on the table.
