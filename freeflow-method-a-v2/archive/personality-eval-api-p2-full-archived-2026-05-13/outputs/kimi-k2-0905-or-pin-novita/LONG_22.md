## If this voice were a person
This sounds like someone who can’t stop noticing, and more importantly can’t stop narrating the noticing. They’d be the friend who turns a late train ride or a weird pause in conversation into a whole philosophy, but in a way that’s half swagger, half private panic. ## What they would probably talk about again
They’d keep coming back to those in-between seconds: the instant before a door closes, the second a person changes, the half-breath where a scene flips meaning. They’d also keep circling around identity as something editable, and around the idea that ordinary details are actually coded messages if you look long enough.

The metaphors pile up fast, but they’re not airy; they’re sticky, mechanical, fluorescent, slightly ridiculous. There’s a performative intelligence here, but it’s always trying to prove itself on the level of objects and scenarios. The voice likes to sound like it has discovered a secret physics of daily life, and it keeps testing that secret against public transit, grocery stores, and insomnia.
