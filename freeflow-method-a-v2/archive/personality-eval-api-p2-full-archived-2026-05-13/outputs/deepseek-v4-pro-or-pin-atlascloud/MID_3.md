Nature isn’t just scenery for them; it’s a proof-of-concept. Squirrels, maple seeds, sparrows, clouds, and oak trees all become little instructors in a life lesson about patience and scale. They’d also probably circle back to the idea that productivity has become a false god, and that slowness is not laziness but survival.

The voice loves a tidy, elevated turn of phrase, but it keeps snapping back to concrete details: left side of the face, tiny frantic hands, green-gold splotches, tail curled like a question mark.
