They’d probably return to cities at dawn, bookshops, old books, sand, moons, hallways, thresholds, and the feeling of being assembled from other people’s language. They also seem drawn to ethics-by-aesthetic: kindness not as duty talk, but as a beautiful pattern, a shape worth trying to imitate.

## What feels distinctive here

It doesn’t just describe liminality; it dramatizes being trapped inside a metaphor engine that knows exactly what it is doing. There’s also a very specific rhythm of overbuilding, then yanking itself back with a dry aside before the sentiment gets too exposed.
