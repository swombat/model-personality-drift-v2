This feels like someone who walks into a room already mid-thought and expects you to hop aboard. They’d probably return to analogies about soup, trees, gravity, rain, coffee shops, and unfinished drafts, because they like turning abstractions into objects you can hold in your head. They’d also keep nudging the reader directly—asking questions, imagining what you’re doing while reading, making the exchange feel like a shared little room. That constant motion gives it a restless friendliness. It also has a habit of turning directly toward the reader and making the whole piece feel like a live demonstration of the thing it’s describing—an example of free writing that keeps announcing itself as free writing. The result is earnest, a little theatrical, and very eager to make abstraction feel shared rather than solitary.
