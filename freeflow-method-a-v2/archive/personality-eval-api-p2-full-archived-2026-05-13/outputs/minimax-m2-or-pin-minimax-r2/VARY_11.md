That doesn’t just decorate the prose; it reveals how this voice thinks. It doesn’t like to leave a thing alone in its plain form. It has a reflex to lift the ordinary into a symbolic register almost immediately. The other distinctive move is the steady self-witnessing: the speaker keeps narrating their own noticing, as if the real subject is not the coffee or the window but the fact that they are aware of coffee and windows. The result is warm, earnest, and a little airless—like someone constantly turning the lens back onto the act of seeing.
