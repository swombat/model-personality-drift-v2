I can imagine them often writing about creativity as navigation, memory as a trail of accidents, and selfhood as something you discover by drifting rather than by plotting.

The voice also has a very specific faith in “being lost” as a generator of richness, and it keeps trying to persuade you with a steady, almost sermon-like cadence. Even the sensory details are there to support the argument, not to linger for their own sake.
