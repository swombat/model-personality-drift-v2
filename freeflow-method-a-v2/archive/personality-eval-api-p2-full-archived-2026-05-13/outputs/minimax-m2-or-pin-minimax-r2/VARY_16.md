## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is how relentlessly it turns everything into a pulse, a rhythm, a breathing thing. Even objects that don’t normally feel alive—smartphones, towers, streets, fountains—get folded into the same heartbeat language, so the whole piece feels like one long attempt to find a single organizing music underneath the city. Another distinctive habit is the smooth glide from the immediate scene to a big abstract claim, without ever really stopping to prove the bridge; it just trusts the sentence to carry itself. The voice also likes paired opposites—fast and slow, mechanical and organic, fleeting and infinite, what was and what could be—which gives it a steady, balancing sway. It’s not sharp or comic; it wants coherence, spaciousness, and a kind of glowing continuity between outer world and inner life.
