They’d also circle back to the idea of silence not as peace but as sterility, and to the feeling that contemporary life is too smooth, too sealed, too efficient to let much of anything echo. If they write more, I’d expect more about listening, memory, and the small physical evidence that time has passed somewhere.

## What feels distinctive here

The last line lands because the whole piece has been building toward a craving to make any kind of mark at all—one small noise, one floorboard in an empty room. That gives the voice a slightly haunted, slightly engineerly feel: lyrical, but with screws showing.
