## What they would probably talk about again
They’d keep circling language itself: what words can do, what they can’t, how naming is both a rescue and a trap. They’d also return to the small physical proofs of living—warm mugs, dust in sunlight, bread, roots, skin, the sound of a neighbor mowing, the pressure of a hand on a shoulder. Big-scale things matter too, but only if they can be made touchable: the universe as an ocean, time as a river, consciousness as a spark, that sort of move. I can picture them repeatedly coming back to memory, grief, fear, and gratitude, but always with a need to pin those feelings to concrete objects or scenes so they don’t float away into abstraction.

## What feels distinctive here

And the ending move is very telling: after all that almost-hymn language, it closes by giving away the leftover words and leaving a blank. That reads as someone who likes the gesture of restraint just as much as the gesture of abundance.
