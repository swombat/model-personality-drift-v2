## What feels distinctive here

Nothing is allowed to just be a kettle; it has to be a duet, an anthem, a ritual, a bridge, a doorway, a seed. That can feel overripe, but it’s also consistent and strangely sincere. The voice doesn’t joke much, and it doesn’t really argue with itself; it keeps smoothing the world into a sequence of gentle correspondences. There’s a hunger here for coherence, for the sense that life is narratable and quietly meaningful, even when nothing dramatic is happening.
