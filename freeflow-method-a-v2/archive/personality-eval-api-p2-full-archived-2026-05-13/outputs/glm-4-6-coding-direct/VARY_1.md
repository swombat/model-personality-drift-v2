They’d probably keep asking whether anything is really gone or just transformed, and whether moving away is escape or a kind of repair. If they told stories, they’d be stories where place and relationship are welded together so tightly that one can’t be thought about without the other.

There’s also a clean, deliberate rhythm to the sentences, like the speaker wants each line to land with a little echo. Even the bleakness is arranged, almost curated, which makes the voice feel composed rather than raw.
