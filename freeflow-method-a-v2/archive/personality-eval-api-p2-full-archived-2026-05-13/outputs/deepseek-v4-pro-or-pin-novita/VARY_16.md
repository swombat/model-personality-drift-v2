They’d also circle aging and self-doubt a lot: noticing gray hairs, feeling older than their parents in old memories, suspecting adulthood is mostly improvisation. The other standout is how it mixes high-flown literary reflexes with very physical little anchors—the blinking cursor, the lower back ache, the smell of rain, the eyebrow hair, the subway ride. So the result isn’t solemn in a heavy way; it’s more like an anxious, articulate mind trying to prove to itself that attention can still mean something.
