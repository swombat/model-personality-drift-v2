The voice loves a lush pile-up of images, but it’s disciplined enough to make them land in waves rather than chaos. There’s also a specific habit of giving tiny things—dust motes, a wasp, a gate, a clock—an almost noble interior life, as if nothing is allowed to be merely what it is.
