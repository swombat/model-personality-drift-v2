There’s also a love of exact texture—ozone, grease, salt spray, xenon-white light, subsonic rumble—which makes the whole thing feel engineered rather than dreamy. It’s not trying to be subtle; it’s trying to make the reader feel the weight, the noise, and the responsibility all at once.
