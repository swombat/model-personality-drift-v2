They’d also probably circle back to the same small objects and images—the mug, the thimble, the rain, the tulips, the paused milk-pour—because they like giving symbolic weight to tiny physical details. There’s also a habit of making abstract ideas feel tactile—“negative space of the conversation,” “disturb them,” “paused the narrative”—which gives the prose a very self-aware, slightly performance-poetry feel. It wants to seem calm while quietly insisting that the room is full of hidden machinery.
