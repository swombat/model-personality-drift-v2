## What feels distinctive here

It loves chain reactions of metaphor: rust becomes time, silence becomes weight, a train yard becomes civilization, a leaf becomes an ethics lesson. The prose keeps climbing outward in big spirals, but then snapping back to one tactile detail—a smell, a timetables pile, vines through a window, dust in the light. That push-pull gives it its particular charge. It’s not just “poetic”; it’s insistent, accumulative, and faintly theatrical, like someone building a cathedral out of warnings and consolation at the same time.
