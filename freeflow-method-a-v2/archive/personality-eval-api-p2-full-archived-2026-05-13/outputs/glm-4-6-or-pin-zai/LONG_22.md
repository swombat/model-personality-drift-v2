## What feels distinctive here

The voice loves repetition with a twist—“it wasn’t just X; it was Y”—and uses that to keep inflating the scene until it feels like scripture written by an engineer. There’s also a very specific pleasure in making the unnatural feel orderly: color-coded voids, manual logs, the rhythm of maintenance, then a sudden swarm of living, singing, bioluminescent repair. The voice doesn’t just want wonder; it wants wonder to have plumbing.
