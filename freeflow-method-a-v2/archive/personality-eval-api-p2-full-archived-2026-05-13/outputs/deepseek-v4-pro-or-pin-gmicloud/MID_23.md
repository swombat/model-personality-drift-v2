## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the constant self-revision while writing: it narrates the act, comments on the act, then comments on the comment. It has that recursive “I know this is what I’m doing, and I’m going to tell you exactly how I’m doing it” rhythm. Another distinctive thing is the oddly precise blend of high-level theory with very specific imagery—“seagulls bickering over a dropped french fry,” “pages made of dried rain,” “potential tenses.” That mix keeps the voice from feeling purely academic or purely dreamy; it’s like someone who uses imagery as evidence. There’s also a subtle dependence on explanation as a form of control. Even when it gets imaginative, it wants to map the trick before moving on. The result is a voice that feels methodical inside its own looseness.
