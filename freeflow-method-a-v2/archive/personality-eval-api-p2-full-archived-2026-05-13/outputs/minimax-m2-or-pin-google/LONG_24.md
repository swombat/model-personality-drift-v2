Another distinctive thing is how often it balances opposites without resolving them—technology helps and harms, solitude is good and lonely, pain is messenger and enemy, joy is small and loud. That balancing act gives the piece a practiced, self-correcting rhythm, like someone thinking out loud and catching themselves before they harden into certainty.
