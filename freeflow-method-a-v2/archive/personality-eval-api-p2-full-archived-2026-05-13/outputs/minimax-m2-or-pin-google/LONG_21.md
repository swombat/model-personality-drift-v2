They’d probably also return to the idea that kindness isn’t just nice but efficient: apology, gratitude, gentleness, and “resumption” all treated like tools that keep systems from breaking down. ## What feels distinctive here

Almost everything gets translated into a second language of mechanism: attention becomes architecture, rest becomes repair, minutes become currency, practice becomes gardening, choice becomes leverage, kindness becomes strategy. It’s not just that the text loves metaphors; it loves metaphors that behave like diagrams. The repeated “Simple examples:” move also gives it a very coached, classroom rhythm, as if it can’t trust an idea until it’s been pinned to three everyday scenarios. There’s a strong preference for reassuring equivalences—if you do X, then Y will follow—so the whole piece feels like a steady hand smoothing out uncertainty into legible steps.
