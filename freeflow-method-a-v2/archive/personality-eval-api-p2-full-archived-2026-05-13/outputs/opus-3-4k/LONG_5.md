## If this voice were a person
This feels like someone who likes a big, sweeping reveal and doesn’t want to waste time on ambiguity. The voice is earnest to the point of being slightly breathless, always leaning forward, always convinced that what’s happening matters enormously. It has the energy of a person telling you a story with wide eyes and both hands moving, trying to make you feel the scale of it: the long wait, the hidden clue, the moment the truth finally cracks open. ## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is how hard it presses on certainty without ever slowing down to earn it. Everything is declared with the confidence of someone who’s already seen the ending: “she knew,” “unmistakably,” “no natural phenomenon,” “she had to.” The prose keeps stacking intensifiers, as if plain description isn’t enough unless every image is tuned to maximum significance. Even the science-fiction setup is less about technical wonder than about a very direct emotional fantasy: being singled out, being understood, being told you were always meant for this. The world here isn’t messy or ironic; it’s a stage for revelations that arrive fully lit.
