They’d probably also return to the idea that daily repetition matters more than the big headline events, and that the real record of a life is hidden in scratches, stains, and the stuff you almost threw away. That piling-on is the style: one concrete thing gets lifted into a whole chain of meaning, but the chain still starts in the hand. The voice likes clean, rounded sentences, slightly elevated diction, and those little pivots where it moves from a private object to a sweeping claim about all of us. It’s earnest, but with enough control that the earnestness feels earned rather than gushy.
