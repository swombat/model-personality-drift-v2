There’s a strong habit of turning sensory detail into metaphor, then stacking one metaphor on another until the whole scene feels a bit ceremonial but still physical. What makes it work is that the voice doesn’t just admire nature; it keeps trying to reclassify reality itself, as if the right phrasing could restore contact with the world. That gives it a hybrid feel: half cabin journal, half sermon to the senses, half self-help for a restless brain.
