Science and technology are clearly catnip here—stars, black holes, protein folding, brain interfaces, the blur between mind and machine—but always in a way that quickly turns back toward what it means for a human body and mind to notice, remember, and choose. Another distinctive thing is the almost constant urge to justify free writing itself—to explain why rambling matters, why noticing matters, why not filtering every thought matters.
