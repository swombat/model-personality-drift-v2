## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the way the voice makes thrift look almost sacred without ever becoming sentimental about it. That’s the signature move—taking ordinary saved junk and making it feel both funny and grave at once, without splitting into separate modes. The voice also has a knack for admitting its own failure to follow through, but in a way that turns procrastination into a philosophy rather than a confession.
