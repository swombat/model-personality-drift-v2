## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the way this voice keeps making the personal sound essay-like without losing the temperature of actual feeling. It has a habit of stacking examples in threes, widening one small observation into a whole human condition, and then landing on a polished line that sounds like it wants to be remembered. There’s also a very specific rhythm to the pressure it applies: it starts with a scene or a feeling, names the cliché, then rescues the cliché by insisting it still holds truth. That move shows up over and over. The writing likes clean binaries that are then complicated—soft/hard, seen/performed, hope/despair, presence/autopilot—but it doesn’t stay messy for long; it likes to bring things back into a legible moral shape. It believes in the depth of people, the inadequacy of words, and the idea that paying attention is almost a form of courage.
