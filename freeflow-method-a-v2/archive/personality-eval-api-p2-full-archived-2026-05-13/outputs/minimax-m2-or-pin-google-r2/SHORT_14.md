It keeps moving, stacking image on image, with a calm confidence that the city is basically an instrument and the people inside it are part of the sound. There’s also a nice refusal to overstate the beauty of it all: “It isn’t magic. It’s weather.” That line tells you the voice likes wonder, but wants it grounded in something physical and shared.
