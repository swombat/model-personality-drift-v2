## What feels distinctive here

It doesn’t just notice that the light looks nice; it promotes the light into evidence about existence, then wraps that in a second layer of reflection about memory, then ends by making the whole room sound like the universe’s engine room. The prose is lush, but the real habit is accumulation: one image leads to another in a steady spiral, and each paragraph adds a new philosophical altitude while staying anchored to sensory specifics. It also has a certain love of neat oppositions—linear vs. circular, fixed vs. revisable, photograph vs. lived moment—and then softens them by insisting the answer is both/and.
