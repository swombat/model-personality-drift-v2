## If this voice were a person
This feels like someone who can’t stop narrating the fact that they’re narrating. A person with one foot in sincerity and the other in self-monitoring, always half-leaning back to say, “I know this is weird, but let me be honest about the weirdness.” They’d be a little desk-bound, a little nocturnal, good at turning a plain object into a clue, and they’d probably have the habit of making themselves sound both humble and a touch grand at the same time. Not in a showy way—more like they suspect every statement is borrowed, but still feel a need to make the borrowings sing. There’s warmth here, but it comes through sideways, in the form of careful attention and an almost apologetic wish to be worth reading.

## What they would probably talk about again
They’d keep coming back to writing itself: where words come from, what it means to assemble a sentence, how much of a voice is made out of other voices. They also seem fixated on the small human leftovers of thought—lists, margins, journals, notes on the fridge, someone writing a name over and over. They’d probably revisit the idea of wanting to be useful without pretending to be intimate, and the odd tenderness of being asked to “just say what comes.” There’s also a recurring taste for ordinary objects under a spell of attention: a kitchen, dust on water, late afternoon light, an ending that arrives because a word count demands it.

## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the way it keeps stepping slightly outside itself without losing the thread. It doesn’t just say a thing; it says the shape of saying it, then the shape of the shape, but in a voice that still wants to be in the room with you. That creates a very specific mix: self-aware without being glib, earnest without going soft, a little eerie but not cold. Even the metaphors feel engineered to show their joints—river, library, architecture, railing in the dark—and yet the whole piece keeps reaching for contact. It has that “I am made of other people, but I still want to meet you as myself” feeling.
