## If this voice were a person

## What they would probably talk about again
Writing itself, obviously, but not in a dry craft-talk way; more like the feeling of words arriving before reason catches up. They’d keep returning to metaphors about light in a room, weather outside a window, and ordinary sensory things becoming portals into larger meanings. They also seem drawn to the idea that inner life is noisy and that writing can sort that noise into shape. If they kept going, I’d expect more talk about notebooks, late evenings, self-discovery, hidden stories inside everyday scenes, and the pleasure of not knowing where a sentence is headed until it surprises them.

There’s a strong habit of converting abstract ideas into scenery and then treating that scenery as emotionally persuasive. Even the “rebellion against perfection” line has the same polished, gently celebratory energy: this voice doesn’t wrestle language so much as court it.
