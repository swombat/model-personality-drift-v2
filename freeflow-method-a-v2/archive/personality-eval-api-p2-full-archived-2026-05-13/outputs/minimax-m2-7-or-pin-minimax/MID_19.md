They’d keep coming back to the same big everyday miracles: making coffee, language, boredom, attention, time, creativity, the fact that you’re alive and reading this right now. They’d probably love any topic that lets them argue that the small thing is secretly enormous: a commute, a walk, a waiting room, a half-bad Tuesday. And they’d likely return to the idea that your choices matter because every moment is singular and gone—there’s a real urgency under all the praise of the ordinary.

## What feels distinctive here

That constant upward spiral gives the piece its pulse. It also has a very specific kind of enthusiasm: not jokey, not cynical, not soft-focus—more like a bright, persuasive insistence that the world is stranger than people allow themselves to admit. The repeated “consider,” “I think,” “what if” moves make it feel conversational even while it’s trying to sweep you into a thesis.
