They’d probably also return to the contrast between being “productive” and being alive, because that feels like one of their favorite little sermons. And they’d keep reaching for moments where something brief becomes emotionally oversized: steam, breath, rain, humming, a hand growing too big, a life passing too fast. They have a habit of making the everyday feel ceremonious without getting sloppy about it. Also, they keep returning to themselves as a slightly comic failure at presence—tourist, distracted mind, trying imperfectly—which makes the voice feel both polished and approachable. The sincerity is real, but it comes wrapped in good phrasing and a sense that the writer enjoys having a thesis and a scene at the same time.
