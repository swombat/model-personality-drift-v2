So they’d probably keep arguing that a scanned copy of a thing is not the thing, and that the cheap, messy, failing version is often the one that actually contains the human part. That mixture gives it a very particular rhythm: grand pronouncements anchored by inventory-level specificity. It likes lists, chambers, labels, measurements, and then suddenly it breaks into a line that sounds almost sacred in its plainness. The whole thing feels built by someone who believes memory lives in residue, not in the official record.
