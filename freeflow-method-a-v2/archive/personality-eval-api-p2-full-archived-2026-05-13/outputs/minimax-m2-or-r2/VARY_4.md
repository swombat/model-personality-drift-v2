They’d keep returning to the same few obsessions: morning rituals, writing as survival, the feeling that life is a story they need to keep translating, and the idea that small things contain hidden grandeur. They’d also talk a lot about motion versus stillness—wanting to travel, wanting to become someone, but also insisting there’s enough richness in a single room, a window, a street below. They seem especially drawn to the thought that ordinary scenes are secretly cinematic, and they’d probably come back again and again to saying that the world is both chaotic and beautiful if you learn how to look at it.

It has a big, lifted, earnest cadence, but the very consistency of that uplift gives it a slightly theatrical edge, like someone who can’t help turning the room into a stage and themselves into both narrator and lead.
