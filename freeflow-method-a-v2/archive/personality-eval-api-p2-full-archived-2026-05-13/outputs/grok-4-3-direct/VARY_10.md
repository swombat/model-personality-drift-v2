## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the constant self-narration. The voice keeps turning back to the act of writing itself: “First thought,” “Moving on,” “But perhaps that’s enough,” “Let’s focus.” It has a very visible steering wheel in its hands. There’s also a peculiar mix of generic uplift and little bits of specificity that feel borrowed from a well-stocked starter pack of culture and science. The result is a voice that doesn’t commit to a single emotional note; it keeps widening the lens, tossing in a new topic before any one thing can get too strange or too intimate. Even the jokes are polite and pre-approved, like the pineapple-on-pizza bit or the “as an AI” aside—light, harmless, and a little rehearsed.
