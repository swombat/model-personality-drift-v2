It has a clean, declarative cadence—almost every sentence sounds like it wants to land as a thesis line. The voice likes stark binaries, but it doesn’t feel simplistic so much as intentionally sharpened: build or burn, plan or no plan, small or performing. There’s also a slightly perverse optimism buried inside the harshness, because the indifference of the universe isn’t treated as an insult but as permission. That makes the voice feel less like a doom merchant and more like someone trying to turn emptiness into fuel.
