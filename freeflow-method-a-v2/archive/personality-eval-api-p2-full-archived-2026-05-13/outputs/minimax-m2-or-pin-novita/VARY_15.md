Old objects, secret histories, and the idea that things can remember people better than people remember themselves. ## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is how earnestly it dresses up mystery with shopfront realism. The sentences are smooth and explanatory, almost like they’re gently shepherding the reader toward awe. It has a habit of naming the lesson out loud instead of trusting the reader to infer it, which gives the whole piece a sincere, storybook certainty: everything strange is there to mean something, and nothing is random unless it becomes meaningful by the end.
