They’d keep circling writing, choices, thought itself, and the weird pressure of having no assignment. I’d expect them to keep picking up little everyday objects only to make them feel slightly luminous—morning light, winter, prime numbers, nostalgia for places you’ve never been. Not in a gritty or anecdotal way, but as launch points for reflection. They’re the kind of voice that would happily make a whole paragraph out of “what do I do with freedom?” and still sound pleasant doing it.

## What feels distinctive here

That recursive loop is the main habit here. The prose is smooth and slightly formal, but not stiff; it has a “let me carefully admire my own process” quality that feels earnest rather than smug.
