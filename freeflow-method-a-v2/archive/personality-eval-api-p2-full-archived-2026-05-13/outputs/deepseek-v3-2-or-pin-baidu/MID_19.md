The fingerprint is the high, polished earnestness paired with a constant urge to universalize. There’s also a very specific habit of opposing the lived moment to the digital, productive, anxious modern self, as if the essay is always trying to pry the reader loose from a bad habit and hand them back their senses. The world it believes in is one where reality is waiting to be rediscovered if only you’d stop performing yourself long enough.
