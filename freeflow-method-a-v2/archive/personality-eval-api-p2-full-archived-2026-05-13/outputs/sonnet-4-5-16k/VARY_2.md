## What feels distinctive here
The little fingerprint is how the writing keeps turning sensory detail into a kind of quiet argument. There’s also a nice habit of making the abstract feel tactile without getting fancy about it—the line from doorknob to life decision to “I’m still here” is very much this voice’s move. Another distinctive thing is the low-key intimacy with self-contradiction: “I’ll fix it this weekend. Or maybe I won’t.” That shrug at the end keeps the whole piece human. It doesn’t need a big revelation; it trusts the unfinished, slightly neglected thing to carry the point.
