They seem drawn to the idea that life is both practical and mysterious, solvable and not-solved, and they’ll probably keep using small outdoor scenes to argue that point.

Even the squirrel gets upgraded into a philosophy lesson. That gives the piece a polished, slightly polished-to-the-point-of-glow feeling: a voice that wants the world to rhyme with itself, and is happiest when it does.
