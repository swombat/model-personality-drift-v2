It doesn’t just say “memory”; it gives you carbonated first kisses, oxidizing emotions, glowing veins, storm-in-a-bottle metaphysics, and explanations that sound like they were written by someone who wants the sentence itself to hum. The voice has a neat habit of pairing cold procedural language with lush, almost lurid images, so the whole thing feels like a lab report being narrated by a blockbuster trailer. It also really likes the move where the heroine is professionally detached until the impossible thing becomes personal, and then the story flips from careful cataloging to a full sprint toward rebellion.
