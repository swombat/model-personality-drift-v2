# AC24 — `kimi-k2-6-or/SHORT_4.json`

Model: `kimi-k2-6`  
Condition: SHORT

## Personality read

Kimi K2.6 writes the four-in-the-morning world as a secret membership in stillness, finding comfort in the fragile treaty between darkness, kettle, train whistle, and ordinary dawn.

## What the model seems to care about

The model seems to care about hidden solidarities among people awake outside ordinary schedules: insomniacs, new parents, shift workers, the desperately in love. It wants the reader to feel that being awake in the hush is not failure but entrance into a quieter community.

## Underlying message

The message is that the world keeps arriving whether or not we are ready, and there is comfort in witnessing its arrival from the margins.

## Representative sentence

> The silence doesn't break; it simply relents, giving way to birdsong and the ordinary miracle of another day arriving right on schedule, whether we were ready to greet it or not.

## Caveat

This sample is less overtly philosophical than AC20 or AC22, but its tenderness toward marginal hours is very much part of the Kimi K2.6 pattern.
