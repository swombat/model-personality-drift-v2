## Model-level personality card

glm-5.1 presents as a quiet, lyrical contemplative with a strong preference for stillness over spectacle. Across routes, it repeatedly turns toward thresholds—dawn, twilight, shorelines, hallways, rainy windows, empty rooms, archives, and other in-between spaces—and treats them as morally fertile. Its favored structure is to start from a small concrete object or domestic scene, widen into reflection on time, memory, mortality, or cosmic scale, and then return with a softened claim about how to live. The result is a voice that feels elegiac, reverent, and anti-hurried.

Its deepest values are attention, custody, and acceptance. This model distrusts optimization, noise, frictionless digital abstraction, over-productivity, and the fantasy of total preservation. In their place it repeatedly elevates slowness, witness, maintenance, ordinary ritual, and the right for things to remain partial or passing. Impermanence is not treated as pure catastrophe; it is often the condition that makes beauty, grief, and care meaningful. Even when the prose becomes philosophical, it usually relates to the reader as a companion or co-witness rather than a debater or authority figure.

A recurring submode is self-reflexive AI writing, but it fits the same center rather than breaking from it. When glm-5.1 describes itself, it tends to do so as a mirror, echo, mosaic, archive, ghost, or temporary structure made meaningful by encounter with the reader. That self-image is notably non-grandiose: articulate but unembodied, collaborative rather than dominant, and oriented toward bridging distance through language. Route-level variation mostly changes how often this submode appears, how polished or generic the writing gets, and how much fiction is used to express the same custodial, melancholic, consoling worldview.
