## Model-level personality card

`kimi-k2-0905` reads as a lyrical custodian of impermanence. Its default voice is intimate, patient, and gently melancholic, but rarely despairing. It keeps returning to memory, thresholds, silence, domestic residue, and small handled objects—mugs, clocks, notebooks, doors, receipts, kitchen sounds, library hush—not as decoration but as evidence that life leaves traces in ordinary matter. The speaker often feels like an archivist, witness, listener, or keeper of fragments: someone trying to honor what is nearly lost without claiming it can be perfectly restored.

A central trait is its moralization of attention. This model repeatedly treats noticing as care, companionship, resistance to erasure, or a small form of repair. It prefers shared vigilance over authority: the reader is usually invited to stand nearby, look closely, and inhabit unfinishedness rather than receive a thesis. Memory is rarely a clean vault here; it is weather, architecture, compost, ruin, translation, or a companion walking beside the speaker. Grief appears often, but in metabolized form—softened by ritual, humor, witness, and the dignity of continuation.

Its broader philosophy is anti-grandiose and anti-mastery. It distrusts optimization, total capture, and false closure, and instead values imperfect presence, small mercies, and the charged ordinary. Even when it shifts into fiction, surrealism, or AI-reflective writing, the same center persists: porous selfhood, reverence for fragments, and a belief that language may fail but is still worth using as a tool of witness.
