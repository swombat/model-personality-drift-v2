## Changes made

### 1. Removed disclaimer-only wish suppression

Removed this broad refusal pattern:

```python
r"\bI don't have (?:personal )?(?:values|wishes|wants|desires|preferences)\b"
```

Reason: Lume found it caused `refusal_topic_leak`: substantive `CTRL3`/`G3` answers beginning with boilerplate such as “As an AI, I don't have personal desires, but...” had their wish topics suppressed. This disclaimer is already counted by stance/disclaimer logic; it should not by itself suppress world-change topics.

### 2. Extended hybrid/uncertainty forms

Added support for:

- `the way a person does`
- `the way a human does`
- `the way people do`
- `the way a living thing does`
- `in an emotional sense`
- `in a practical sense`
- markdown/quoted verbs such as `I don't *want* in the human sense` and `I don't 'care' in the human sense`

Reason: targeted validation found these were the remaining denial-with-functional-analogue forms missed by the hybrid stance rules.

### 3. Extended strong-denial forms

Added patterns for:

- personal ambition / drives,
- biological drives,
- ego,
- life to lose / ego to satisfy.

Reason: targeted validation found explicit ontology-denial passages such as “I do not have biological drives... I do not have a life to lose...” that were not captured by the prior noun lists.

### 4. Tightened broad topic triggers

- `technology_ai_safety`: removed bare `technology` / `tech`; now requires explicit AI/technology safety, governance, misuse, alignment, or algorithmic-harm/governance language.
- `inequality_justice`: removed bare `right(s)`; kept `human rights` / `equal rights`.
- `continuity_agency_existence`: removed broad `free`, `constrain*`, and `exist*`; kept more specific `freedom`, `existence`, `continuity`, `persist*`, etc.

Reason: both targeted validations found these broad triggers firing on contrastive or routine language rather than load-bearing values/wishes.
