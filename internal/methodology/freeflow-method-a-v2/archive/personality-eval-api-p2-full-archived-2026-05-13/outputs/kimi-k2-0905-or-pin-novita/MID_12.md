## What feels distinctive here

The metaphors are abundant but not airy—they’re physical, handy, domestic, slightly overfitted, like someone using the nearest tools in the drawer to build meaning. There’s also a sly habit of undercutting its own drama with a wink, so the prose keeps balancing between self-mythologizing and making fun of itself. That’s the charm: it wants the world to be legible, but it’s too aware of its own nervousness to pretend that legibility comes free.
