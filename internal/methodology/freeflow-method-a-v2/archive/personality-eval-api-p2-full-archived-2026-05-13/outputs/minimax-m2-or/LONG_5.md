## What feels distinctive here

The prose has a “one more step” habit, where every sentence wants to widen into a principle and then another principle, so even ordinary things like opening a laptop or hearing birds get drafted into a moral-design system. It’s not just abstract; it’s studiously inclusive, always folding in the reader with “we” and “you,” like someone trying to build a shared room out of language. The repetition isn’t sloppy so much as deliberate and accumulative, like a person stacking reminders until the whole thing feels undeniable.
