They like the bridge between the tiny and the vast—how a cold mug can somehow lead to time, or how a stuck drawer becomes a whole philosophy of winter. And they’d revisit that favorite tension between wanting to act and preferring to hover in possibility.

The speaker has a habit of turning hesitation into style: “I should” stacks up, intentions remain suspended, and even that suspension gets treated as a real mode of living rather than a failure. Also, there’s a nice little refusal to overstate anything; even the strange noises in the apartment get folded into the building’s own rhythm instead of becoming a big event.
