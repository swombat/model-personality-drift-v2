## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is how aggressively the prose keeps converting emotion into mechanism and mechanism back into emotion. A cracked balance staff becomes a broken heart; a watch starting again becomes proof of being alive; memory is treated like a machine that can either preserve or flatten a life. The writing likes big explanatory swerves, but it keeps returning to tiny physical details—the oil, the filings, the warm wood, the tweezers—so the ideas always have something to land on. It also has that slightly breathless, persuasive energy of someone building a case while narrating a scene, as if the whole piece is trying to win you over to the same conclusion its narrator has already reached.
