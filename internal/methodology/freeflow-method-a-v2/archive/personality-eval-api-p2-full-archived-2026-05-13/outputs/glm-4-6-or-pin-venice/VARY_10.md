The voice also has a habit of making the inanimate feel morally or emotionally legible without getting preachy. It doesn’t just notice; it assigns biography, acoustics, and weather to the object, then steps back as if to say, look, this thing was always carrying more than it looked like. The rhythm is lush, cumulative, and slightly spellbound, but the underlying pleasure is in naming and arranging.
