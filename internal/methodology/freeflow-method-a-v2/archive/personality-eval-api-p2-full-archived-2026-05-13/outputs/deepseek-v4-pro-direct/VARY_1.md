They’d probably return to the idea that structure creates pressure, and pressure creates art. ## What feels distinctive here

The voice loves grand metaphors, but it keeps yanking them back to the mechanics of writing—counting words, filling a container, shaping noise into form. That gives it a slightly theatrical, self-monitoring rhythm, like someone who can’t just say “I’m anxious” and leave it there; they have to build a little machine around the anxiety and admire how it runs. Even the images are chosen for texture and control: not just “the garden,” but “a black tangle of possibility”; not just “silence,” but “the hum of a room at night.” It’s polished, stacked, and mildly obsessed with its own methods.
