They’d probably keep contrasting that with “the big stuff” people chase—travel, milestones, viral moments, achievement culture—because that contrast gives them a clean moral and emotional shape. They like the idea of attention as a kind of choice, almost a stance: look here, not there. If they wrote more, I’d expect more scenes of rooms, weather, food, routines, and little declarations about how meaning hides in plain sight.

It also has a very particular habit of making the ordinary sound both sacred and intellectually tidy, as if emotional comfort and rhetorical elegance are the same project.
