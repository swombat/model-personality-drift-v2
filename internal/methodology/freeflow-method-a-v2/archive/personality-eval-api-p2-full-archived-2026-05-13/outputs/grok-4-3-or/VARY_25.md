## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the way it keeps announcing its own structure while it’s building it: “let’s begin,” “what else comes to mind,” “let me expand,” “let’s list random thoughts,” “to reach word count.” It’s not trying to hide that it’s improvising. That gives the piece a very manufactured spontaneity, like a person trying to sound natural by narrating the mechanics of being natural. Another distinctive thing is the little bursts of mild, generic wit—“I don’t drink, but data shows caffeine’s effects” —which feel like a polite machine trying on casualness. It never really sinks into one mood; it keeps changing lanes before the reader can settle, and that constant switching becomes the style.
