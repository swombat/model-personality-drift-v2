It gives the impression of someone who thinks in prepared statements, where even sincerity has been ironed smooth. There’s also a very specific habit of balancing pairs—digital and organic, fast and slow, self and other, private and public—so the voice feels committed to symmetry, as if every thought needs a mirrored half to look complete. The result is less diary than manifesto for a tasteful, high-functioning interior life.
