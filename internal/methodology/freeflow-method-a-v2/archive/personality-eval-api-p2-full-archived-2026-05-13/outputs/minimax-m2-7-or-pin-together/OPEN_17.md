## If this voice were a person

They’d keep coming back to walking, weather, cities, and the feeling that small details are secretly messages. They’d also return again and again to writing itself as a metaphor for travel: the blank page as a city square, the pen as a shoe, the sentence as a step. I’d expect them to repeatedly say that freedom is not external but internal, and to keep trying to prove that with a fresh image every time.

## What feels distinctive here

It’s not just “poetic,” it’s hyper-assisted—everything arrives already interpreted, as if the voice can’t bear to let a streetlamp just be a streetlamp. There’s a buoyant overcommitment to making the world glow with meaning, but also a neat, almost storybook tidiness to it: even “grief” gets turned into music, drizzle becomes applause, and the ending lands on a line that sounds like it was written specifically to be quotable. The voice wants to keep lifting the scene upward, into polish and resonance, more than into mess or surprise.
