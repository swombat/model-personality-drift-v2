It’s earnest without being frantic, polished without sounding playful, and it has that specific habit of making ordinary scenery feel like it’s quietly auditioning to become a life lesson. Even the stranger on the bench feels less like a character than a delivery mechanism for a sentence the narrator can keep in their pocket.
