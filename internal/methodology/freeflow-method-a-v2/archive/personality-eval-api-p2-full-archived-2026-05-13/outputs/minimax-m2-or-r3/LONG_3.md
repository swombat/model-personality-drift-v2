## What feels distinctive here

What makes it feel specific is the rhythm: a soft confession, a broad cultural complaint, then a polished return to the personal anecdote as if the anecdote were the proof. It’s not edgy, ironic, or sharply idiosyncratic; the fingerprint is that steady, earnest over-articulation, the sense that the writer trusts repetition and expansion to do emotional work. Even the self-awareness—“I’ve been thinking about this,” “Let me tell you”—feels like part of the style, a way of gently taking your hand and leading you through the idea.
