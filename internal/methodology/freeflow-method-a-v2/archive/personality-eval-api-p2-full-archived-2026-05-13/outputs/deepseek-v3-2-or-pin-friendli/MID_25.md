## What feels distinctive here

That gives the piece a very specific sheen: it wants to sound careful, a little lyrical, and emotionally legible without ever saying something bluntly plain. There’s also a neat little oscillation between the intimate and the generalized—“my grandmother’s bowl” slides quickly into a claim about art, memory, and humanity as if the personal example is just the doorway to a bigger thesis. It has a restrained warmth, but it’s the warmth of someone who likes ideas enough to let them stand in for confession.
