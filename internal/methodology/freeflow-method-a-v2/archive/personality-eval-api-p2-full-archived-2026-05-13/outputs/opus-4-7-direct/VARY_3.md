## What feels distinctive here
The little fingerprint is the way it keeps stepping half a pace away from its own beauty. It notices something, then immediately comments on the noticing, like it’s trying not to get caught being poetic. That self-interruption gives the piece its shape: sincere image, then qualification; intimate thought, then a little systems-level aside; a neat metaphor, then a shrug about whether the metaphor belongs to the speaker at all. It also has this very specific habit of making the abstract feel concrete through one tiny scene—the man with peas, the ducks, the cursor, the kitchen window. The voice doesn’t roar; it gathers. And even when it talks about uncertainty, it does so with a strange calm, like uncertainty is just another material to arrange on the page.
