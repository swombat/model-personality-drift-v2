## If this voice were a person

There’s almost no clowning here, but there is a dry self-awareness: they know they’re doing a Very Serious Thing about sketches and incompletion, and they keep calling attention to the fact that they’re doing it.

I can easily imagine them bringing up books, letters, shelves, buildings, old notebooks, and any object that lets them say: the process is the point, not just the product. They’d likely also keep worrying at the same bigger question from different angles—how to live without forcing everything into a clean ending.

## What feels distinctive here

Even the self-reference is part of the style; it’s comfortable saying “what I’ve written here is itself a sketch,” which makes the whole piece feel like someone who wants their prose to embody the point instead of merely explaining it.
