They’d also probably return to the tension between settling into daily life and not letting that become an excuse for passivity. The imagery is soft-edged and reliably golden, but the real habit is the moral tidying-up—every scene gets turned into a principle. Even when it names a danger, it does so in a way that reinforces the original position. The result is a voice that wants both intimacy and coherence, and is always just slightly in love with its own ability to find depth.
