Earnest to the point of pressure, a little polished, very sure that clarity and uplift are part of the job. They’d keep circling the same cluster of “important” things: learning, creativity, attention, distraction, self-improvement, and how modern life either helps or sabotages the mind. There’s a strong “essay voice” here that loves clean transitions, numbered advice, and elevated but familiar phrases; it wants to sound wise without getting weird. The sentences are fluent and confident, but they have a slightly prepackaged sheen, like a polished motivational explainer that believes earnestness plus vocabulary equals depth. Even the closing move is characteristic: it comes back to the blinking cursor and makes the whole thing about the act of writing itself, as if the essay has to prove its own thesis in real time.
