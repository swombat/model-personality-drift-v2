The prose likes tactile specifics—bergamot, bluebells, damp wool, periwinkle paint—but it uses them almost like high-resolution anchors for a floating, reflective voice. There’s also a slightly cultivated prettiness to the metaphors and a confidence that the speaker’s inner associations matter enough to be narrated in full. It feels like someone who is always half-writing the essay version of their own life.
