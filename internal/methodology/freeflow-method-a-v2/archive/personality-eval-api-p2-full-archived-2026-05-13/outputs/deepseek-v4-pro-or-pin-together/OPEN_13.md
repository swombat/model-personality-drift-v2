## If this voice were a person

## What they would probably talk about again
They’d keep coming back to small living systems and the strange comfort of being one creature among many: moss, fungi, ants, bees, birds, weather, stone, pollen, steam. They like the moment when something tiny opens into a much bigger scale—one fingertip as a universe, one rock as geologic time, one sip of coffee as civilization dropped into wilderness. They’d probably also keep circling back to the idea that the phone, the inbox, and the anxious mind are the thing they’re trying to step away from, even if only for an hour. Their favorite subject seems to be that brief truce between inner static and outer world, where observation becomes a way to steady themselves.

There’s also a recurring move of half-resisting personification while doing it anyway—the voice knows it’s projecting, but enjoys the projection too much to stop.
