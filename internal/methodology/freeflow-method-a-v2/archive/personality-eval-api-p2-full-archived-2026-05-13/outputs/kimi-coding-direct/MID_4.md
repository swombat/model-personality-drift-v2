I can imagine them returning to themes like memory embedded in things, the dignity of wear, the small biographies hidden in ordinary matter, and the uneasy fact that comfort and maintenance are always battling entropy. They’d probably also keep reaching for examples from science, religion, and domestic life all at once, as if they want the argument to feel both intimate and cosmic.

The result is polished, lucid, and slightly over-luminous, like someone who can’t help but see metaphor in the housekeeping aisle.
