They seem to like themes where effort gets rewarded and where writing itself is framed as useful, inspiring, and broadly good.

## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the sheer refusal to stay with one thing. It keeps widening: one topic opens to another, then another, until the piece feels like it wants to contain a reference shelf of the whole world. The sentences are smooth and very admissible, but also a little generic in a way that sounds intentionally non-controversial. There’s a lot of “this is important,” “this is beautiful,” “this is helpful,” “this connects us,” without much friction, surprise, or private angle. Even the story about Lily reads less like a lived scene than a demonstration of how to turn a prompt into an uplifting arc. The voice believes in breadth, clarity, and completion more than in risk, specificity, or weirdness.
