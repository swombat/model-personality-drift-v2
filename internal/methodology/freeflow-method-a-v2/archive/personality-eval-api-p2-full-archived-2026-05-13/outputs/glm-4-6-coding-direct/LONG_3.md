## What feels distinctive here
The little fingerprint is how relentlessly the voice dignifies the discarded second without ever sounding shy about it. It also has a playful habit of pretending to be omniscient, then narrowing down to one named person with a very specific tiny moment, which makes the whole thing feel half cosmic lecture, half museum tour. The emotional move is always the same and still effective: start by sneering at human blindness, then end up deeply affectionate toward it.
