They’d also keep circling the same contrast between isolation and company—how quiet can feel clean at first, then start to warp the mind, and how even a bad storm can feel more alive than safety. If this person kept writing, they’d probably be obsessed with places that put you at the edge of things: cliffs, towers, ships, empty roads, dark water, any setting where the world feels stripped down to wind and mechanism.

Even the self-awareness is theatrical: “I exist” lands like a tiny flourish, not a confession. The voice likes to stand at a remove and narrate itself as if it were already a legend.
