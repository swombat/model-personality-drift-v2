If they were in a conversation, they’d likely drift from a decaying building to memory, grief, classification, and the weird relief of things slipping out of view. They’d probably bring up cities, transit, maps, and the tension between planning the future and letting the past dissolve.

Another little signature is the way it treats forgetting not as failure but as a kind of release, while still sounding slightly guilty for enjoying that release. It’s mournful only in a controlled, essayistic way, with a strong taste for architectural metaphors and the pleasure of naming things precisely.
