## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the voice’s habit of making a sermon out of materials science without sounding holy about it. The metaphors are muscular and a little theatrical—“parliamentary assembly,” “republic,” “bookkeeping,” “great leveler”—yet the piece never floats away from grit, skin, soot, ash. It’s also unusually fond of turning nouns into political bodies and natural processes into civic institutions, which gives the whole thing a sly, stately engine. The rhetoric keeps layering: one sentence is domestic, the next is cosmic, the next is archival, and somehow the transitions feel earned because the writer keeps insisting that matter itself is the bridge.
