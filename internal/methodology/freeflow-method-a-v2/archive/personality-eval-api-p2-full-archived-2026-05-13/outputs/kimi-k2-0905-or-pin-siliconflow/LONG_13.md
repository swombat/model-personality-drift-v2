## What feels distinctive here

The voice loves a list, but not as dry enumeration—more like a spell built from inventory. It has a very particular move of starting with a concrete object and then sliding, almost imperceptibly, into cosmic argument: a pocket becomes privacy and power, a laundromat becomes parallel universes, a cloud becomes a lesson in refusal, a deadline becomes a line where time can be shot dead. That glide from tactile to metaphysical is the signature. Also, it has an almost performative confidence in its own analogies—sometimes funny, sometimes gorgeous, sometimes a little too pleased with itself—but that swagger is part of the charm.
