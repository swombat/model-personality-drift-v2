## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is how insistently the voice turns “getting lost” into both an argument and a mood, while still keeping one hand on the railing. It’s not just romantic talk about wandering; it keeps correcting itself, qualifying the privilege, naming the downside, then swinging back to the longing anyway. That back-and-forth gives it a very particular rhythm: admire the mystery, warn against the naïveté, then propose the practice. The prose likes tidy triads, rhetorical questions, and little escalations from personal anecdote to cultural history to philosophical takeaway. It believes in maps, but it believes more in the meaningful things that happen when maps fail.
