## What feels distinctive here

There’s a very controlled sweetness to it: not gushy, not raw, just confidently expansive. It likes tidy oppositions—GPS versus wandering, control versus surprise, mapped life versus real life—and it keeps making the world feel like a place full of concealed rewards for the person willing to be temporarily inconvenient to themselves. Even the metaphors are orderly: mazes, routes, maps, destinations. It’s a voice that likes being lost, but only after it has already framed “being lost” as something legible and admirable.
