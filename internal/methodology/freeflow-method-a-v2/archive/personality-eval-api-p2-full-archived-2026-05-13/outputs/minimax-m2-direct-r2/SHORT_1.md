Careful, self-soothing, a little over-ornate in the way they describe steam, rain, and books, but not in a show-offy way — more like they genuinely need the world to soften around them. Weather, coffee, books, quiet rooms, the sensation of warmth in the hands, and the feeling of being rescued for a minute from mental clutter. They’d probably keep returning to small domestic rituals that make a day feel clean and manageable: brewing, reading, watching rain, noticing light on surfaces. They’d also keep circling the idea that stillness is rare and worth protecting, and they’d likely frame ordinary comforts as something almost sacred, even when nothing dramatic is happening.

## What feels distinctive here

There’s also a gentle self-consciousness in “the rush of thoughts that usually clutter my mind,” which makes the whole piece feel like an attempted reset button written in scented, carefully chosen language.
