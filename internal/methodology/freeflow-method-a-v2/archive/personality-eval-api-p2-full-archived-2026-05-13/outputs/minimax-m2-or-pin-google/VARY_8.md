## What they would probably talk about again

Music seems like their favorite hinge between then and now. They’d also probably keep building little relic-hunts: a chest, a sealed letter, a forgotten single, a name that suddenly matters.

## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is the constant conversion of sentiment into prop and texture. It stacks lush metaphors fast, but not randomly; everything is there to make remembering feel tactile and slightly staged, like a private myth being reassembled in a shop after hours. The other tell is that it treats objects as if they have agency and patience—records waiting, the city remembering, a song digging up the past.
