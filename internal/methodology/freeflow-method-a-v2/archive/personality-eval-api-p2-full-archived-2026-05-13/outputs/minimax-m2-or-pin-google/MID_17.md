## What they would probably talk about again
They’d keep circling writing itself, but also the etiquette of making things: what makes a sentence “right,” what tools help and what tools cheapen the work, what freedom really means when it still has to answer to form, audience, and discipline. They’d also probably keep bringing up the idea that ease can be suspicious if it skips the struggle that teaches you how the thing is made. There’s a recurring urge to defend slowness, revision, and the dignity of handwork—even when the subject is something abstract like AI or freedom.

Even when it’s talking about wandering, it’s already organizing the wander into three small sentences, a circuit through the neighborhood, a folder of images. The voice likes to make a claim, then soften it with an image, then widen it into a rule about life, democracy, or craft. It feels earnest, but not bluntly earnest—more like someone who wants the reader to feel the thought arriving, not just hear the conclusion.
