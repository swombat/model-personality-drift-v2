They’d also keep returning to the moral side of expression—what you owe other people when you tell a story, when silence is kinder than speaking, when a sentence bridges or walls someone off. They seem especially drawn to the ordinary mechanics of making meaning: the click of keys, the act of trimming a paragraph, the way a word changes shape in another person’s mind.
