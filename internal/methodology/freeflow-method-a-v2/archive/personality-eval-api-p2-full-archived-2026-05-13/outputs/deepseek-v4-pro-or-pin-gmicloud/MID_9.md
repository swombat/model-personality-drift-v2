They’d likely return often to the idea that small things are not small if you look at them long enough, and that what ages us is also what gives us texture. They’d probably complain about modern distraction too: notifications, headlines, the general racket of being alive now. But even that complaint would come out as a carefully made sentence, not a rant.

Everything gets recast through a chain of image, then image of image, until the desk is a diary, dust is a galaxy, silence is a kind of sound, and memory is a surface that can be scratched and worn smooth.
