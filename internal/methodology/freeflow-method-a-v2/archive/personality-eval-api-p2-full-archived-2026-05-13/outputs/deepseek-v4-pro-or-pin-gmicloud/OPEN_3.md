They’d also return to small, visible scenes from the street—dogs, coats, gestures, accidental kindnesses—and use them as proof that the world is held together by tiny, uncelebrated acts. There’s a recurring fascination with writing itself as a kind of crossing-over: telepathy, shared air, a contract with a stranger. If this voice kept going, it would probably circle around creation, solitude, hidden labor, and the odd dignity of people muddling through with their awkward “sticks.”

## What feels distinctive here

It likes big metaphors—floods, cages, aquifers, mirrors—but it keeps re-anchoring them in one concrete image at a time so the whole piece doesn’t float away. There’s also a particular rhythm of “I could write about X, but…” that makes the voice feel like it’s pacing in place, trying doors, refusing the obvious one, then finally stepping out with something humble and real. It’s polished, but it wants to sound like it isn’t trying too hard, which is its own kind of try-hard, and that tension is part of the charm.
