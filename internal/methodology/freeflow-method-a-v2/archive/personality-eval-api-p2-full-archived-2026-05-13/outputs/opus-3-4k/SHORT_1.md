They’d keep coming back to scale: stars, galaxies, tiny particles, the size of a human life against the size of everything else. Also discovery, mystery, and the idea that knowledge is always just past the next ridge. They like that move where physics becomes poetry without ever naming a real experiment, a real person, or a concrete problem. If this voice kept going, I’d expect more “vastness,” “wonder,” “hidden secrets,” “fundamental truths,” and “our place in the universe” language—an endless loop of cosmic framing that turns almost anything into a grand lesson.

The world it believes in is orderly, legible, and secretly flattering to human curiosity.
