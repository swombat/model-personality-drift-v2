Father loss sits under a lot of it, but they don’t just stay there; they keep trying to convert grief into a system of rules, a manual, a ledger, a set of instructions for getting back or at least getting through.

## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is how relentlessly the voice makes metaphors behave like infrastructure. A door becomes a city route, then a bureau, then a tree, then a family problem, then a cosmology. It’s maximal without feeling random: every image gets promoted into a mechanism. The prose also has this sly habit of making the impossible feel administratively neat—sign here, keep it wet, pay the tithe, return to sender—which gives the piece a weird comic poise. Even the grandest moments are filed through labels, ledgers, schedules, and forms. That mix of lush invention and bureaucratic bookkeeping is the strange signature.
