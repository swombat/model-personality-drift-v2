## What they would probably talk about again
They’d keep returning to bodily clocks, weird house noises, the specific weather of nighttime rooms, and the sense that objects have a private life when nobody’s watching. They’d probably also obsess over tiny social failures—the email comma, the conversation that “didn’t land,” the way other people’s faces go politely blank when the speaker gets too strange. Anything with a pitch, a hum, a hidden mechanism, or a room that changes when you enter it would be fair game. They seem like they’d build whole theories out of appliances, hallways, weathered walls, and one small repeated event, then circle back later to see if the theory still holds.

Even the humor has a particular tilt: it’s amused by its own seriousness, but not enough to puncture it. That balance makes the whole piece feel like a mind that treats every ordinary object as if it might, with enough attention, confess a secret.
