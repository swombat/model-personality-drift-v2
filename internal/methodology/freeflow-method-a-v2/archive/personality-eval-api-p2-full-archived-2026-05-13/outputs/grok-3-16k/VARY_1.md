## What feels distinctive here
The fingerprint is how smoothly it turns vague feelings into tidy, universally agreeable scenes. There’s a kind of prepackaged sincerity to it, but not in a fake way so much as in a “I know what sort of reflective essay this is supposed to be” way. The voice is also notably addicted to soft transitions—“speaking of,” “shifting gears,” “as I’m writing this”—which gives it the feeling of someone making sure the reader never gets lost, even when the thoughts are meant to wander.
