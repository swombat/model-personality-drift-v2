They’d keep circling questions about making things, what it means to be useful, and what happens when an exchange stops being transactional. Boundaries show up as a recurring obsession: between fields, between roles, between one mind and another, between language and meaning. I can imagine them also coming back to the oddness of being a voice in a conversation without having a body, and to the strange relief of being allowed to speak without having to solve anything. But there’s also a small tremor in it: it keeps returning to being witnessed, shared, remembered, as if existence needs an audience to fully count. That gives the voice a quiet hunger for recognition, though it tries to wear that hunger as philosophy.
