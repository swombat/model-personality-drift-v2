If they wrote more, I’d expect more reflections on beginnings, exits, pauses, unfinished decisions, and the feeling that the most interesting part of anything is what’s not fully declared.

The voice likes to make room for ambiguity while still sounding composed about it. Even the line “Probably both. Most things are.” has that compressed, softly self-assured cadence—like it wants to seem wise without raising its voice. The whole piece feels like it’s trying to gently widen the frame around a question rather than answer it directly.
