They’re ornate but not chilly; they like saying the same idea three different ways until it clicks into place, and they clearly enjoy the slow turn from physical detail to bigger meaning. ## What they would probably talk about again
Unfinished things, definitely: abandoned books, half-built selves, old drafts, conversations that never happened, rooms in a house that are always there but never quite resolved. They seem especially drawn to the emotional afterlife of attempts — not success or failure, but the weird charged space where something almost happened and then didn’t. I’d expect them to keep returning to houses, doors, basements, windows, shelves, kitchens at dawn, and any object that can carry the sense of a life paused mid-action. They also have a thing for rescuing the word “unfinished” from shame and making it sound almost noble, like the truest parts of a person are the parts still under construction.

## What feels distinctive here
