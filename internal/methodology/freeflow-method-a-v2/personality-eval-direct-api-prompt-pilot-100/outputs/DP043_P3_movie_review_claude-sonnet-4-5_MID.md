## Sensibility

The relationship to the reader is intimate in a practical way: it keeps saying “you,” drawing the reader into the machinery, making the claims feel testable against private experience rather than delivered from a pedestal.

## Camera keeps pointing at

Even the “video recording” comparison shows the voice’s target: it wants to dismantle the fantasy of clean capture and replace it with something messier, more human, and more useful.

## The world according to this voice
In this world, a person is not a stable container but a running draft. Events do not stay intact; they get touched by attention, mood, gossip, photos, and the years that come after. A childhood scene can remain vivid for no obvious reason, while a major life event goes soft around the edges. Forgetting is not a defect but a necessary clearing-out. Shared history is not truly shared, only overlapping. Even a false memory can matter because it changes how someone moves through the day. The voice treats the mind as practical rather than sacred: memory exists to help you get on with being alive, not to preserve a perfect archive. It likes facts, examples, and the mechanics of how things work, but it keeps returning to one plain, unnerving idea: what feels most like “you” may be a story assembled from scraps.
