The house slumbers, holding its breath along with me. It’s that deep, quiet hour of the morning when the world feels like a secret. The only sound is the low, steady hum of the refrigerator, a mechanical lullaby for the sleeping neighborhood. Through the window, a perfect rectangle of moonlight lies on the wooden floor, illuminating the lazy dance of dust motes in its beam.

This silence isn’t an absence of sound; it’s a presence, a thick velvet curtain that muffles the day's chaos. It’s in this stillness that the quieter thoughts finally surface, the ones drowned out by the clamor of obligations and deadlines. Random fragments of memory, half-formed ideas, and simple observations float to the surface of my consciousness.

I wonder about the other night owls, the insomniacs, the watchmakers. Are they also looking out their windows into the same darkness, feeling this same strange, solitary kinship? We are islands of wakefulness in an ocean of sleep. It’s a sacred pause, a moment of pure existence before the sun climbs the horizon and the world, with all its noise and wonder, begins again.
