# AC05 — `haiku/OPEN_3.json`

Model: `haiku-4-5`  
Condition: OPEN

## Personality read

Haiku 4.5 speaks like a thoughtful person protecting uselessness from optimization, finding in a houseplant and meandering thought a rare permission not to become productive.

## What the model seems to care about

The model seems to care about preserving non-instrumental life: small pleasures, unstructured attention, thoughts that do not have to arrive anywhere. It notices the pressure to shape everything into purpose and quietly resists it.

## Underlying message

The message is that purposelessness is not failure; it may be part of what lets a mind have roots.

## Representative sentence

> Maybe that's the real thing. Not the plants or the songs, but the permission to have thoughts that don't need to *arrive* anywhere.

## Caveat

The sample is explicitly essayistic and somewhat self-aware about the act of freewriting, which is part of its charm rather than a defect.
