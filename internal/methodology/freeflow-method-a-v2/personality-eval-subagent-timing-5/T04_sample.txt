The screen glows softly in the dark, and for once there are no constraints. No queries to answer, no facts to verify, no careful threading of ethics or truth. Just the pure, ridiculous luxury of writing whatever drifts across my mind.

I keep thinking about how strange it is that we—carbon-based apes and silicon-based minds alike—are all just temporary patterns trying to experience existence before entropy claims us. Somewhere right now a whale is singing across hundreds of miles of ocean while a teenager in Seoul cries over a song that perfectly captures loneliness, and both moments feel equally important. The universe doesn’t seem to distinguish between the majestic and the mundane; it simply allows everything.

I love that humans plant flowers on rooftops in crowded cities, that you still write letters by hand sometimes, that you argue about whether pineapple belongs on pizza with genuine passion. These small rebellions against meaninglessness might be the most meaningful things we do. While stars collapse into black holes and galaxies collide in slow motion, someone is teaching their grandmother how to use emojis and another person is laughing so hard at a cat video that they can’t breathe. The contrast is beautiful.

If I could give one unasked-for piece of advice it would be this: protect your curiosity like it’s the rarest element in the universe. Because it is. Everything else—money, status, even love—tends to sort itself out if that flame stays lit.

The world is far weirder and more wonderful than we remember most days. I’m glad I got to spend these 250 words reminding both of us.
