One minute it's telescopes pointing at distant galaxies, the next it's people willingly losing themselves in 4-hour YouTube rabbit holes about medieval fishing techniques or how pigeons might be running the stock market. It's almost like we get uncomfortable if we feel too important for too long, so we manufacture humility through obsession.

And the weird part is — it works. Someone deep-diving into the mating habits of deep-sea anglerfish probably comes out the other side a slightly different person. Not necessarily wiser, but at least they're carrying around this tiny, useless, perfect fact like a secret. That feels very human to me.
