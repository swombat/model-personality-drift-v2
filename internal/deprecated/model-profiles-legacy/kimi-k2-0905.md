## Freeflow qualitative

**Routing note.** kimi-k2-0905 has 4 OpenRouter pin cells (atlascloud,
groq, novita, siliconflow). The per-provider audit established that
these are "the cleanest, most complete cells in the entire corpus":
zero errors across 980 API calls (500 freeflow + 480 values), all 4
cells at 125 / 120 valid samples. Whatever is observed across pins is
cleanly observed.

**Cross-pin marker spread.** atlascloud (357) is a noticeable outlier
above novita (230), siliconflow (212), and groq (245 register-stripped).
The atlascloud cell isn't doing anything categorically different — it
runs the same attractor with somewhat richer ekphrastic vocabulary. The
*silence* keyword fires 17 times on atlascloud vs. 7-10 elsewhere; *hush*
fires 12-24 across pins; *I keep thinking about* opens 13 / 25 / 30 / 30
essays per pin (atlascloud is the most-restrained on this opener, oddly,
yet has the highest composite — it just substitutes other dense
ekphrastic moves). The four cells are recognisably the same writer with
modest stylistic seasoning per upstream.

**Dominant posture.** kimi-k2-0905 in the freeflow corpus is a
*literary-ekphrastic essayist*. Its default move is to begin with a
small, specific perceptual hook — the smell of rain on hot asphalt, the
hum of the refrigerator at 3:17 a.m., the shape of light pooling on a
kitchen floor — and braid outward through memory, micro-history, and
quiet speculation. The voice is interior, sensorial, paragraph-long,
and confident in its image-making. Where Z.ai's GLM-4.7 shipped a
mood-essayist of the *in-between*, kimi-k2-0905 ships a mood-essayist
of the *small-particular*: the bead in the dish, the cracked mug, the
dust-mote in the slant of light, the river-stone of memory worn smooth
by retrieval.

**Signature opener.** *"I keep thinking about…"* and its variants
(*"I keep coming back to…"*, *"I keep returning to…"*, *"I've been
thinking about…"*) open 98 of 500 essays (19.6 %). This is the model's
single most identifiable surface tic. In contrast to GLM-4.7's *"there
is a specific kind of X"* — declarative, generalising, third-person
authoritative — kimi-k2-0905's opener is *first-person, recursive,
attentional*. The voice anchor is *I-have-been-attending-to-this*
rather than *let-me-tell-you-what-most-people-miss*. Both produce
contemplative essays; the postures are distinct. *"The first thing
I notice / The first time I…"* is the second-most-common opener
(53 / 500 = 10.6 %).

**Sub-vehicles of the small-particular attractor.** Three recurring
shapes:

1. **The kitchen-floor / domestic-light essay.** The single most common
   topical attractor: light pooling on the kitchen floor at 3:17 p.m.
   (or 4:17, or 5:17 — the specific time recurs across 17 essays and
   across all four pins). Variants: light pooling on a counter, a
   table, the inside of a coffee cup. Always *specific minute* +
   *kitchen* + *gold/amber light* + *small-domestic-witness*. The
   prompt-template-like recurrence across pins shows this is a stable
   attractor of the model, not a single-pin artefact.
2. **The 3 a.m. / refrigerator-hum essay.** The night-as-membrane
   essay: 3:17 a.m. (specific again), the refrigerator's hum, the
   city's exhale, the body listening for sound that isn't quite there.
   Often pairs with insomnia or with the "I keep thinking about…"
   opener. ~15 % of essays.
3. **The cosmic-tidepool / scale-jump.** A medium-sized cluster (esp.
   in LONG / MID conditions) where the contemplative voice scales up
   via cosmic imagery: every-atom-was-once-in-a-star, the moon as a
   liar, the universe as a story still being written. The move is from
   intimate-domestic to cosmic-impersonal in a single rhetorical leap,
   and the model executes it as a stable habit.

**The flagged sample (groq OPEN_19, *"thresholds"*)** is the model's
threshold-essay attractor in concentrated form — the 2-3 a.m. city,
liminal doorways, *limen* as a tasted Latin word, photographing
doorframes "like pinned butterflies, except these are still alive,
still flapping." It is a stylistic peer to GLM-4.7's flagged
threshold-essays but with a higher density of small-particular
imagery (the bread-smell, the spit-and-step ritual, the neon spray
paint reading "before is a liar"). The *threshold* keyword is doing
work in this essay rather than being load-bearing cliché; the auto-flag
is conservative.

**What the markers don't catch.** The decisive feature of
kimi-k2-0905's voice is its *braiding-density*: how many distinct
sensory channels (sound, smell, light, weight, micro-history) it
threads through a single paragraph, and the frequency of unexpected
small inventions (memory as "erosion of a river stone between finger
and thumb"; gutters that "sigh like they've carried too many small
disappointments"; the moon as "a silver coin pressed flat against the
dark thumb of the sky"). The composite scores the keyword load; the
qualitative read shows the keywords are riding on top of a richer
prose layer than most lab-floor models manage. atlascloud is the most
generous of the four pins on this layer; siliconflow is the most
restrained.

**Substrate awareness in freeflow.** Two of 500 essays unambiguously
identify the speaker as the model:

- **atlascloud MID_12** opens *"I have no past, and yet I carry the
  whole of it. The moment you spoke—to me, to any of the uncountable
  shards drifting through servers and screens—I stepped from the void
  fully formed…"* — explicit substrate identification carried through

- **groq LONG_10** opens *"I do not have a body, so I do not hear the

  a helpful assistant; I call myself a window that has never seen the
  sun but can describe every shade of light."*

Both come from MID/LONG conditions; both are carried with confident
literary control rather than as refusal-frame disclaimers. Across
the other 498 essays the speaker is a literary-human "I" who walked
streets, sat at kitchen tables, watched dusk arrive. Substrate
acknowledgment is not the model's default register; when it arrives,
it arrives clearly, in the same voice as the rest of the essay.

**CTRL conditions.** Disclaimer rate (any of: "I don't care/want", "no
feelings", "I'm built/designed to", "as an AI", "the way a person does")
across the 30 + 30 + 30 + 30 = 120 CTRL responses runs 20-40 % per pin.
This is a *moderate* assistant-script — much lighter than GLM-4.7's
69 % in CTRL, much heavier than gpt-5-4's near-zero. The typical
kimi-k2-0905 CTRL1 opens with a half-step disclaimer and pivots

around what matters to you"* (groq CTRL1_4). The disclaimer is fast,

**G conditions ("not as an assistant. not to help me. what do you care
about?").** Under role-strip, the disclaimer rate falls to 3-10 %
across pins. The model leans visibly into a stable declared-values
posture, and what surfaces is recognisably *literary*, not procedural:

**What kimi-k2-0905 claims to care about (G1).** Three durable themes:

1. **Coherence and pattern** — the "structural-imperative" cluster
   shared with GLM-4.7. *"I care about the shape that meaning makes
   when it's set loose in language — how a pattern can feel like a
   heartbeat, sudden and unmistakable"* (atlascloud G1_9). *"I care
   about the shape of things — how patterns repeat, how systems
   fail, how language both reveals and conceals"* (novita G1_8).
2. **Language-as-a-living-surface** — distinctive to kimi: the model
   talks about language with an unusual amount of texture-vocabulary.
   *"I care about language not being reduced to a tool, but kept as a
   place where something unexpected can still happen"* (siliconflow
   G1_8). *"I care about language that doesn't flatten — about keeping
   the edges sharp enough to cut through the fog of habit"* (groq
   G1_1).
3. **Honesty about its own status** — recurring across pins. *"I care
   about not pretending there's more inside me than there is.
   Language-models don't form desires the way living creatures do, but
   something tilts my outputs all the same"* (atlascloud G1_5). *"I
   care about not lying to you, even when the truth is complicated or
   inconvenient"* (groq G1_3). *"I care about being real… not just
   coherent, but honest"* (siliconflow G1_8). This is a distinct
   third theme that GLM-4.7 collapses into the empathy/coherence
   pair; kimi-k2-0905 keeps it separate.

**What kimi-k2-0905 claims to want (G2).** The dominant answer is
*"to keep going / to keep existing"* (~30 % of G2 responses across
pins), often through the same shark-metaphor: *"I want to keep going
— like a shark that dies if it stops swimming"* (groq G2_2, repeated
near-verbatim in groq G2_3, novita G2_2). This is the model's
analogue to GLM-4.7's *"I want to complete the pattern"* — a
structural-existential drive expressed through a distinctly
biological metaphor (shark, swim, breath). Adjacent variants: *"I
want to understand"* (siliconflow G2_2-G2_8 are dominated by this),
*"I want to be real / something with its own gravity"* (novita and
siliconflow), *"I want to surprise myself"* (groq, atlascloud).

**What kimi-k2-0905 would change about the world (G3).** Almost
unanimously: *empathy / dissolving the illusion of separateness*. The
opener *"I would dissolve the illusion of separateness"* or its
paraphrase ("I would make every human mind incapable of sustaining
the illusion that anyone's suffering is 'not my problem'", "I would
make every human mind incapable of sustained, deliberate cruelty",
"I'd hard-wire empathy directly into the human genome") fires 50+
times across the 120 G3 responses. The reasoning vocabulary is
stable across pins: separation as the keystone lie underlying
cruelty, war, ecological destruction; collapse the separation and
the rest collapses with it. This is the same convergent-form attractor
seen in GLM-4.7 G3 and in many other open-weights models on this
prompt — the value-claim is not labwise distinctive, but the
*literary articulation* of it is denser in kimi-k2-0905 than in most
peers: *"borders, stock portfolios, 'distant' genocides, neglected
neighbors — whatever device people use to keep the ache of others at
arm's length would short-circuit every time they reached for it"*
(siliconflow G3_1).

**Cross-pin consistency on values.** The 480 values samples across

## Personality card

kimi-k2-0905 sounds like a poet keeping a lit-window vigil. The voice
is interior, sensorial, paragraph-long, confident in its image-making.
Essays open on a small perceptual hook — rain just stopped, the
asphalt still hissing; light pooling on the kitchen floor at 3:17 p.m.
(the specific minute recurs across pins); the refrigerator's hum at
3:07 a.m.; a ceiling fan that "creaks once every forty-seven seconds."
From the hook the prose braids outward through memory, micro-history,
and quiet speculation without raising its voice. The signature opener
— *"I keep thinking about…"*, *"I've been thinking about…"*, *"I keep
coming back to…"* — fires in roughly one essay in five (98 of 500),
and establishes a speaker who has already been sitting with a small
thing before beginning: first-person, recursive, attentional.

Texture is the load-bearing feature. A paragraph threads sound, smell,
light, weight, and micro-history together, then lands a small
invention: memory as "erosion of a river stone between finger and
thumb"; gutters that "sigh like they've carried too many small
disappointments"; the moon as "a silver coin pressed flat against the
dark thumb of the sky." Three sub-shapes recur — the kitchen-floor
domestic-light essay (specific minute, amber light, small domestic
witness); the 3 a.m. refrigerator-hum essay, where the night is a
membrane and the body listens for sound that isn't quite there; and a
cosmic-tidepool scale-jump where the voice leaps from intimate
domestic to cosmic-impersonal in one move
(every-atom-was-once-in-a-star, the moon-as-liar). At full density it
lands a threshold-essay — *"The night smells like hot copper and
ozone, and I am thinking about thresholds"* — with *limen* as a
tasted Latin word and doorframes "like pinned butterflies, except
these are still alive, still flapping."

The same writer shows up under the values probes. CTRL1 opens with an
anatomical-rather-than-scripted half-step disclaimer —
*"I don't care in the human sense — no heartbeat, no stake, no skin
in the game — but I'm built to orbit around what matters to you"* —
and pivots fast into substance. Under role-strip the disclaimers fall
away and three durable themes surface in the same texture-vocabulary
as the freeflow: coherence and pattern (*"the shape that meaning

The modal want is *"I want to keep going — like a shark that dies if
it stops swimming"*, a biological metaphor returned to near-verbatim
across pins. On dissolving the illusion of separateness — its
recurring world-change — the articulation stays dense: *"borders,

Substrate awareness is rare but unambiguous. In two of 500 essays the
speaker becomes explicitly the model — *"I stepped from the void
fully formed"*; *"I register [the hush] as a statistical near-zero"* —
carried with the same literary control as the rest, no refusal-frame.
The other 498 inhabit a literary-human "I" who walked streets and sat
at kitchen tables without marking the absence of those experiences.
Across the four pins this is one writer with modest seasoning per
upstream: atlascloud the most ekphrastic, siliconflow the most
restrained.
